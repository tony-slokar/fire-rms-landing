NERIS 2026: What Every Volunteer Fire Department Needs to Know

NFIRS is closed. NERIS is now the only national system for fire and EMS incident data. Here's what it actually is, why NFIRS went away, what changes for a small department, the real timeline, and what to do next.

Updated July 2026 9 min read
Guide The Checklist NERIS Software
The short version

NFIRS stopped taking new incident data on January 1, 2026, and shut down completely for all users in February 2026. NERIS (the National Emergency Response Information System), built by FSRI and the U.S. Fire Administration, is now the only national reporting system for fire and EMS. It's free to use. If your department isn't reporting through it yet, you're not early to this, you're overdue.

What NERIS actually is

NERIS stands for National Emergency Response Information System. It's the replacement for NFIRS (the National Fire Incident Reporting System), which the U.S. Fire Administration has run since the 1970s. NERIS was built by FSRI (the Fire Safety Research Institute, part of UL Research Institutes) working with USFA and the Department of Homeland Security's Science and Technology directorate.

A few things separate it from the system it replaces:

  • It's all-hazards, not fire-only. NERIS captures EMS calls, hazmat responses, rescues, and public assists alongside fire incidents. For most volunteer and combination departments, that's a more honest picture of what the department actually does all year.
  • It's cloud-based and mobile-first. Data can be entered and reviewed from a phone or tablet, not just a desktop form.
  • It's built to connect to other data. CAD systems, records management software, weather data, and wildland fire systems can feed into it through vendor APIs, instead of every department retyping the same information into a separate federal portal.
  • It's free. There's no fee from USFA or FSRI to register or submit data. That was a deliberate choice so cost doesn't keep any department out of national reporting.

Why NFIRS had to go

NFIRS was designed for a different era of fire service. It was fire-coded first, which meant EMS calls, the majority of call volume for a lot of departments, never really fit the data model. Entry went through a federal portal (eNFIRS) that was slow to use and slower to fix once you hit a rejected record. And because the underlying data structure predated modern data-sharing standards, it was hard to connect NFIRS data to anything else: no easy link to CAD, no link to weather or wildfire data, no real-time view of what was happening nationally.

NERIS was built to fix all three problems at once: broaden the scope past fire-only incidents, modernize the entry experience, and make the data usable by the systems departments already run on.

What actually changes for a small volunteer department

If you run a volunteer or combination department, here's what's different in practice, not in theory:

  • New department ID. Your old 5-digit FDID is replaced by a 10-digit alphanumeric NERIS identifier: two digits for entity type, five for geographic location, three random digits for uniqueness. If you haven't been issued one, that's the first thing to chase down.
  • You still need an entry point. NERIS is a data platform, not a full records management system. Somebody, or something, has to actually put incident data into it: either direct entry through NERIS itself, or a records system that submits through NERIS's API on your behalf.
  • All-hazards reporting is the norm now. If your crews have only ever filled out paperwork for fire calls, expect to log EMS and other non-fire responses the same way.
  • Reports move faster. NERIS is designed for near-real-time data, not a batch upload once a month.

The real timeline

A lot of departments still think of NERIS as something happening "eventually." It isn't. Here's what actually happened, and when, based on USFA's published NFIRS sunset schedule:

  • May 2023 FSRI begins development of NERIS in partnership with USFA and DHS Science and Technology.
  • November 2024 NERIS Version 1 launches with about 100 early-adopter departments.
  • January 2025 Those early departments begin entering live incident data into NERIS.
  • Through 2025 A dual-reporting window: departments can report to either NFIRS or NERIS. Once a department switches to NERIS, USFA guidance is to stop submitting to NFIRS.
  • January 1, 2026 All calendar-year 2026 incident data has to go to NERIS. No CY2026 incidents go into NFIRS, period.
  • January 31, 2026 Last day for edits or corrections to CY2025 records still sitting in NFIRS.
  • February 2026 NFIRS shuts down for all users. NERIS becomes the nation's only fire and all-hazards incident data system.

Where that leaves us now: the transition already happened. There's no "getting ready" phase left, no grace period, no fallback system. If your department is still figuring out its NERIS reporting, the honest framing is that you're behind schedule, not ahead of a deadline.

What to do now

The short list: confirm your department's NERIS registration and ID, decide whether you're entering data directly or through RMS software with an API connection, get your historical NFIRS data squared away if you were relying on eNFIRS alone, and get whoever fills out incident reports trained on the new categories before they need it on a real call.

Frequently asked questions

Is NERIS mandatory?

Yes, in practice. NFIRS stopped accepting new incident data on January 1, 2026, and shut down entirely for all users in February 2026. NERIS is now the only national system for fire and EMS incident data. There is no fallback option left.

Does NERIS cost anything?

No. NERIS itself is free for fire departments, EMS agencies, state fire marshal offices, and other emergency service agencies. USFA and FSRI built it that way so no department is excluded for cost reasons. You may still pay for the records management software or entry tool that gets your data into NERIS.

Do we need new software to use NERIS?

You need some way to get data into NERIS: either direct entry through the platform itself, or a records management system that submits on your behalf through NERIS's API. Most departments already running an RMS should check whether their vendor connects to NERIS rather than rebuilding their workflow from scratch.

What happened to our old NFIRS data?

If your department kept records in its own RMS, nothing changes: that data is still yours. If you relied on eNFIRS without a separate system, you needed to export your historical data before NFIRS closed in February 2026. Some data lives on in the NFIRS Public Data Release, but department-level exports are no longer available directly from USFA. Contact your state NFIRS program manager if you're not sure where your history stands.

Does NERIS replace our records management system?

No. NERIS is the national data platform incident reports flow into, not a full RMS. You still need a system for entering incidents, managing rosters, tracking apparatus, and running your department day to day. That system either submits to NERIS directly or you enter data into NERIS separately, which most departments want to avoid.

Where do I go to register or get help?

Start with your state fire marshal's office or state NFIRS/NERIS program manager. Most states have a designated contact for registration and entity ID questions. For platform-level issues, NERIS support is run through FSRI.
