NERIS is the national reporting system, but reporting to it is only useful if the data getting there is accurate and doesn't cost your officers a second round of paperwork. That's the part LadderOps is built around. Background on NERIS itself, what it is and why it replaced NFIRS, is in our NERIS 2026 guide.

How LadderOps handles NERIS

Step 1

Enter once

The incident record your crew documents on scene is the same record that gets submitted. No retyping the narrative into a separate reporting portal after the call is already closed out.

Step 2

Guided validation

Required fields and inconsistent entries get flagged while you're still entering the report, not weeks later in a rejection notice you have to go track down.

Step 3

Direct submission

Once a report is complete, it goes to NERIS from inside the platform your officers already use, no separate export, upload, or federal portal login required.

Frequently asked questions

Is LadderOps just for NERIS reporting, or a full RMS?

It's a full records management platform. NERIS reporting lives inside the same system you use for personnel, apparatus, water supply, pre-incident plans, and billing, so incident data doesn't have to be re-entered anywhere.

How long does it take to get a department up and running?

AI-assisted onboarding does most of the heavy lifting. Send us your roster and apparatus lists in any form, even a photo of a handwritten page, and the import maps them into your system for a quick review. One recent department went from paper lists to a fully loaded system in an afternoon.

Do we own our data?

Yes, unconditionally. Export everything at any time in standard formats, with no export fees. If you ever leave, your data goes with you.

What does founding pricing include?

Early departments get the entire platform at $25 per member per year, locked for the life of their subscription, including every module and feature shipped later. Founding pricing ends for new departments only, existing founding departments keep their rate.

Does it work with spotty rural cell coverage?

Yes. The mobile app is offline-first: crews can document in dead zones and everything syncs once coverage returns. It was built around rural Wisconsin response conditions, not a downtown demo room.
