Questions

Straight Answers

What is NERIS, and why does 2026 matter?

NERIS is the national incident reporting system replacing NFIRS. Departments are transitioning now, and manual entry through the federal portal is slow and unforgiving. LadderOps departments enter incidents once and submit to NERIS directly, with validation that catches problems before they bounce.

How painful is switching from our current system?

We do the heavy lifting. Send us your roster and apparatus lists in any form, even a photo of a handwritten page, and our AI-assisted import maps them into your system for a quick review. A recent department went from paper lists to a fully loaded system in one afternoon.

Does Rally replace IamResponding or Active911?

Yes. Rally covers dispatch alerts, one-tap response status, a live status board, and department messaging, and it connects to the same records platform you run the rest of the department on. One login, one bill, one vendor.

Does it work where cell coverage is bad?

Yes. The mobile app is offline-first: crews can document in dead zones and everything syncs when coverage returns. We built for rural Wisconsin, not a downtown demo room.

Who owns our data?

You do, unconditionally. Export everything at any time in standard formats. If you ever leave, your data goes with you. No export fees, no hostage negotiations.

What does founding pricing mean?

Early departments get the entire platform at $25 per member per year, locked for the life of their subscription, including every module and feature we ship later. When founding pricing ends, it ends for new departments only.
