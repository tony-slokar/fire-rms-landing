Privacy Policy for LadderOps

Last Updated: July 9, 2026

1. Introduction

LadderOps provides records management and operational software for fire and EMS departments, including our website (ladderops.tech), our web applications, and the Rally mobile application for responders. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share, and retain information across the LadderOps platform.

2. Our Role — Departments as Controllers

Most information in LadderOps is entered, configured, and controlled by the fire or EMS department (or agency) that subscribes to our services. For that information, the department is the data controller and custodian of its records, and LadderOps acts as a processor on the department's behalf, handling data as directed by the department and under our agreement with it. Your department decides what is collected, who can see it, and how long official records are kept, consistent with its records-retention schedule and applicable law. If you are a responder or department member, questions about your data should generally be directed to your department, and we will support your department in responding.

For information you give us directly outside of a department relationship (for example, through our waitlist or contact forms), LadderOps is the controller.

3. Information We Collect

a. Contact and waitlist information. When you fill out our waitlist or contact forms: your name, work email, department name and size, and details about your current software and challenges.

b. Account and personnel information. When your department sets up accounts: name, work email, role or rank, and personnel details your department chooses to record.

c. Operational and records data. Information your department's users enter to run the department, such as incident and NERIS-aligned fire and EMS reports, apparatus and personnel records, pre-plans, and inspections. Some of these are official public-safety records governed by your department's obligations.

d. Location information (Rally mobile app). With your permission, Rally collects your device's precise location:

  • In the foreground, while you are actively using location features of the app (such as viewing a response map).
  • In the background, only during an active response you have confirmed. When you set yourself as responding to an incident, Rally shares your location with your department's command staff so they can coordinate the response. This background sharing stops automatically after the time period you choose in the app's settings (for example, 5, 10, 20, or 30 minutes), or when the incident is cleared, whichever comes first. You may also choose to have it continue until the incident clears, and you can turn location sharing off entirely at any time in the app or in your device settings.

We do not track your location when you are not responding, and we do not use your location for advertising or share it with advertising networks.

e. Device and notification information. A device identifier and a push-notification token, used to deliver dispatch alerts and messages to your device. Tokens are removed when you sign out or deregister the device.

f. Usage and diagnostic information. Basic technical and usage information needed to operate, secure, and improve the services.

4. How We Use Information

  • To provide the LadderOps platform and the Rally app to your department.
  • To coordinate emergency response, including sharing a responding member's location and status with their command staff.
  • To deliver dispatch alerts and messages.
  • To secure the services, prevent abuse, and troubleshoot.
  • To communicate with you about the service and, where you have opted in, our Early Adopter Program.
  • To comply with legal obligations.

5. How We Share Information

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not use it for cross-app tracking or advertising. We share information only:

  • Within your department — command staff and dispatchers can see responder status and, during an active response, responder location, as configured by your department.
  • With your department's 911 dispatch or CAD system, where your department enables it — for example, to display responding-unit locations on dispatch maps.
  • With service providers who help us operate the platform (such as cloud hosting, Apple and Google push-notification services, mapping providers, and email), under agreements that limit their use of the data to providing those services.
  • For legal reasons — to comply with a legal obligation, respond to lawful requests, or protect the rights, safety, and security of users and the public.
  • In a business transfer — in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

6. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as necessary to provide the services, as directed by your department, and as required to comply with applicable records-retention, public-records, and other legal obligations.

  • Official records (incident, NERIS/EMS, and related operational records) are retained according to your department's records-retention schedule and applicable law, for which your department is responsible as custodian.
  • Location information shared during a response is retained only as long as needed to support that response and any after-action review, and is then deleted or de-identified.
  • Push tokens and device identifiers are removed when you sign out or deregister a device.
  • Waitlist and contact information is retained as long as needed for the purpose you provided it, unless you ask us to delete it sooner.

7. Your Choices and Rights

Location: You control location sharing through the app's toggle, through the "stop tracking after responding" duration setting, and through your device's location permissions. Turning it off does not prevent you from using the rest of the app.

Notifications: You can manage push notifications in the app and in your device settings.

Access, correction, and deletion: Because your department controls most of your information, please direct these requests to your department, and we will assist the department in fulfilling them. For information LadderOps controls directly, contact us using the details below.

Promotional communications: You may opt out of promotional emails at any time. Depending on where you live, you may have additional rights under laws such as the CCPA/CPRA. We honor applicable rights and do not discriminate against you for exercising them.

8. Security

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect information, including encryption of sensitive credentials and access controls scoped to each department. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information and to respond appropriately to any incident.

9. Children's Privacy

LadderOps and Rally are professional tools for fire and EMS personnel and are not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the "Last Updated" date above and, where appropriate, provide additional notice.

11. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us at: info@ladderops.tech.
