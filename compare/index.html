LadderOps sells two things: Rally, a response coordination app for dispatch alerts, status tracking, and messaging, and the LadderOps Platform, a full records management system that includes Rally plus NERIS reporting, billing, water supply, pre-plans, inspections, personnel, and more. Departments evaluating either one usually already have a shortlist of tools they're comparing against.

The pages below put Rally and the LadderOps Platform side by side against the products chiefs ask us about most: response apps like IamResponding, Active911, and RedAlert, and full records management platforms like First Due, ESO Fire RMS, and Fireproof. Every comparison is built from publicly available information, mostly each vendor's own website and published materials, and we say so plainly where a fact isn't published anywhere.

We're not neutral. We build and sell these products. But a comparison page that misrepresents a competitor isn't useful to a chief trying to make a real decision, so we've tried to write these the way we'd want a vendor to write about us: factual contrasts, and honest "choose them if" framing where it's warranted.

Frequently asked questions

How do I pick between Rally and the full LadderOps platform?

If you just need dispatch alerts, response status, and messaging, start with Rally on its own. If you also need NERIS reporting, billing, water supply, pre-plans, inspections, and personnel management, the full platform includes Rally plus all of that. Departments can start with Rally and add the platform later without switching systems.

Are these comparisons neutral?

We built these from publicly available information about each competitor, their own websites and published materials, and we say so where a fact isn't published. We're not going to pretend we're unbiased since we sell LadderOps and Rally, but we're not going to misstate what a competitor offers either. If something here is out of date, tell us and we'll fix it.

What if a competitor isn't listed here?

We're starting with the products departments ask us about most often. More comparisons get added over time. If you want to see a specific one, reach out and let us know.
