LadderOps vs First Due

Both are full fire and EMS records management platforms built to run a department end to end. Here's how NERIS reporting, water supply, pre-plans, billing, AI features, and pricing actually compare, module by module.

LadderOps and First Due are both cloud-based records management platforms built to run a fire department end to end, not just log incidents. Personnel, apparatus, inspections, pre-plans, and incident reporting all live in one system instead of a stack of separate tools. The differences that actually matter when you're evaluating both are how NERIS reporting works today versus what's planned, how AI features are described versus what they specifically do, and what's included in the price you pay versus what gets negotiated separately.

How NERIS reporting compares

First Due lists NFIRS and NERIS reporting with automated compliance exports among its fire incident documentation features, per their site. LadderOps takes a different kind of proof point: NERIS submission is live in production today, not a pilot or a forthcoming release, with two Wisconsin fire departments submitting real incident data through the platform right now. An officer enters an incident once, inside the same record used for personnel, apparatus, and billing, and it goes straight to NERIS from there.

AI: named features vs a general claim

First Due's site describes AI as embedded throughout the platform to automate routine tasks, improve documentation quality, optimize scheduling, and support real-time decision-making. That's a broad claim, and it's not paired with a list of specific capabilities on their site as of this writing. LadderOps names three specific AI features and what each one does: AI onboarding photographs a handwritten roster and returns a mapped import ready for review, an AI form builder turns a state PDF into a digital form that prints back onto the official document, and AI narrative assist drafts an incident narrative from facts already entered in the record. If you're evaluating AI claims from any vendor, ask what the AI actually does and see it run, not just how it's described on the site.

Pricing: one published number vs a quote process

LadderOps publishes one number: $50 per member per year, all-in, for the full platform, NERIS reporting, billing, water supply, pre-plans, inspections, personnel, apparatus, AI onboarding, AI form builder, AI narrative assist, reporting, and Rally included. Founding departments lock in $25 per member per year for the life of their subscription. Optional add-ons, Quartermaster asset management and Controlled Substances tracking, are each $500 per department per year, clearly separate from the base price. First Due doesn't publish pricing on their site; it's a contact-for-quote, enterprise sales model. Third-party industry-estimate aggregators, not First Due's own published numbers, suggest a range of roughly $8 to $25 per user per month, plus separate one-time costs commonly cited for implementation ($3,000 to $20,000), data migration ($1,000 to $8,000), and CAD/dispatch integration ($2,000 to $10,000). Actual quotes vary by department size and modules selected, so treat those figures as context for the conversation, not a number to compare against directly.

Which one fits your department?

Choose LadderOps if...

  • You want one published, all-in price you can budget against without a sales call
  • You're a volunteer or combination department that needs NERIS reporting live now, not on an enterprise implementation timeline
  • You want AI features that do one specific job each, rather than a general "AI throughout the platform" claim
  • Data portability and no export fees matter to you as an explicit policy
  • Rally response coordination bundled with your RMS, at no extra charge, matters to you

Choose First Due if...

  • You're a larger or career department already deep in an enterprise procurement process
  • You need ePCR with NEMSIS compliance and real-time hospital data transfer as part of the same platform, not just fire records
  • You want a resident/business self-service portal (Community Connect) as part of the suite
  • You already have GIS infrastructure and want pre-plans built on top of Esri integration
  • You're comfortable with a quote-based pricing model tailored to your specific module mix

Frequently asked questions

Is First Due's NERIS reporting the same as LadderOps'?

Both support NERIS reporting. First Due lists NFIRS and NERIS reporting with automated compliance exports among its fire incident documentation features, per their site. LadderOps' NERIS submission is live in production today, with two Wisconsin fire departments submitting real incident data through the platform, not a pilot program.

How does pricing actually compare between LadderOps and First Due?

LadderOps publishes one number: $50 per member per year, all-in, or $25 per member per year for early departments on founding pricing, locked for life. First Due doesn't publish pricing directly; third-party industry estimates, not First Due's own published numbers, put it around $8 to $25 per user per month, plus separate implementation costs commonly cited at $3,000 to $20,000, data migration at $1,000 to $8,000, and CAD integration at $2,000 to $10,000. Actual quotes vary by department size and modules selected, so treat those figures as a starting point for questions, not a quote.

Does LadderOps include ePCR or EMS charting like First Due does?

Not today. First Due's ePCR is NEMSIS-compliant with Kno2 integration for real-time hospital data transfer. LadderOps doesn't include EMS charting as part of the platform.

What AI features does each product actually have?

First Due's site describes AI as embedded throughout the platform to automate tasks, improve documentation, and support decision-making, a broad claim without specific named capabilities listed. LadderOps names three specific AI features: AI onboarding that maps a photographed roster into a reviewable import, an AI form builder that turns a state PDF into a digital form that prints back onto the official document, and AI narrative assist that drafts incident narratives from facts already entered in the record.

Is First Due a better fit for a department already invested in GIS-based pre-planning?

It might be. First Due's Pre-Incident Planning module includes Esri GIS integration and aerial photography, which is worth weighing if your department already has GIS infrastructure and wants pre-plans built on top of it. LadderOps includes pre-incident plans as part of the core platform without a separate GIS licensing question.

Can we export our data if we ever switch platforms?

With LadderOps, yes, at any time, in standard formats, with no export fees, as an explicit policy, not just a support request. We're not aware of published export terms from First Due; ask them directly as part of your evaluation.
