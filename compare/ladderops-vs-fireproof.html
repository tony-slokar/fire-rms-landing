Where the AI actually differs

Fireproof's AI is built around the moment of narrative creation and after-the-fact review. Voice-to-text dictation lets an officer talk through what happened instead of typing it. Dispatch audio transcription pulls structured text out of radio traffic. AfterActionIQ, per their site, assists with facilitating an after-action review, and a customer quote on their site credits it with saving "4+ hours of prep time for every after action meeting." An AI chatbot embedded in the RMS lets you ask questions of your own records instead of running a report. That's a coherent bet: reduce the friction of talking and reviewing.

LadderOps's AI is built around three specific, narrower jobs: getting a department's existing paper or spreadsheet data into the system without manual re-entry (AI onboarding), turning a state's official PDF form into something that behaves like the original when printed (AI form builder), and drafting an incident narrative from the structured facts an officer already entered in the record (AI narrative assist), rather than from spoken audio. None of this is voice-driven, and LadderOps doesn't have dispatch transcription, video summaries, or a chatbot today. If dictation or an audio-first workflow is what your officers actually want, that's a real gap worth naming directly rather than glossing over.

NERIS: certification claim vs. production track record

Fireproof states on their site that they are the "First NERIS Certified RMS" and describe themselves as NERIS V1 Data Exchange Compatible. That's their own published claim, and it's a legitimate one to make if the underlying compatibility work is done. LadderOps points to a different kind of proof: two Wisconsin fire departments are submitting real incident data to NERIS through LadderOps in production today, not in a sandbox or pilot. A compatibility or certification claim and a track record of live production submissions are both real, but they answer different questions, one is "can it," the other is "is it, right now." Ask any vendor which one they mean before you take either claim at face value.

Platform breadth beyond AI

Set the AI features aside and the two products cover different ground. Fireproof's published modules are occupancy tracking, training records, hydrant data, investigations, inspections, daily logs, and accreditation reports, plus a PPE and turnout gear lifecycle compliance module aligned to NFPA 1850 that's marketed to both fire departments and independent PPE service providers, a broader audience than a typical department-only RMS. LadderOps doesn't have a dedicated PPE compliance module today. What LadderOps includes that isn't listed on Fireproof's site is cost recovery billing and invoicing, pre-incident plans, apparatus checks and maintenance, and Rally, a response coordination app included in the base platform with one-tap response status, a live status board, CAD dispatch alerts, and department messaging.

Pricing transparency

LadderOps publishes one number: $50 per member per year, all-in, for the full platform, NERIS reporting, billing, water supply, pre-plans, inspections, personnel, apparatus, reporting, and Rally included. Founding departments lock in $25 per member per year for the life of their subscription. Optional add-ons (Quartermaster and Controlled Substances) are $500 per department per year each, clearly separate from the base price. Fireproof does not publish pricing anywhere on their site as of this writing. That's not unusual for a younger SaaS company selling to institutional buyers, but it does mean you'll need a sales conversation before you know what you're comparing on cost.

A note on company stage

Fireproof was founded by Nate Gilbert who, per Fireproof's own published origin story (covered in a PR Newswire release and a Nextworld case study), was previously a CPA who became convinced of the potential of no-code development tools; his grandfather was a firefighter, which the company credits as the motivation behind the mission. The team reportedly built their MVP using no-code tools and launched in roughly three months. That's a fair, neutral fact about company stage, not a knock, plenty of good software starts this way. It's worth knowing you're evaluating a newer vendor either way.

Frequently asked questions

Do both LadderOps and Fireproof use AI?

Yes, both lead with AI as a differentiator. Fireproof's named AI features center on voice-to-text narrative dictation, dispatch audio transcription, an after-action review assistant called AfterActionIQ, AI-generated incident summary videos, and an in-app chatbot for querying your records. LadderOps's named AI features are AI onboarding from a photographed roster, an AI form builder that turns a state PDF into a digital form, and AI narrative assist that drafts a narrative from the facts already in the record. The two products picked different AI problems to solve first.

Does LadderOps offer voice dictation for incident narratives?

Not today. LadderOps does not currently offer voice-to-text dictation, dispatch-audio transcription, or an in-app RMS chatbot. LadderOps narrative assist works differently: it drafts a narrative from the structured facts already entered in the incident record, rather than transcribing spoken or dispatch audio. If dictation specifically is what you need, that is a real gap worth knowing about upfront.

Is Fireproof really NERIS certified?

Fireproof states on their site that they are the first NERIS certified RMS and describe themselves as NERIS V1 Data Exchange Compatible. That is their own published claim. LadderOps takes a different kind of proof point: two Wisconsin fire departments are submitting real incident data to NERIS through LadderOps in production today. A compatibility claim and a track record of live production submissions are both legitimate, but they are not the same thing, and it is worth asking any vendor which one they actually mean.

How does pricing compare?

LadderOps publishes one all-in price: $50 per member per year for the full platform, or $25 per member per year for founding departments, locked for the life of the subscription. Fireproof does not publish pricing anywhere on their site; you have to contact them directly for a quote.

What does Fireproof cover that LadderOps doesn't?

Fireproof includes a PPE and turnout gear lifecycle compliance module aligned to NFPA 1850, marketed to both fire departments and independent PPE service providers. LadderOps does not have a dedicated PPE compliance module today. LadderOps covers water supply and hydrant management, pre-incident plans, cost recovery billing, and Rally response coordination as part of the base platform, none of which are listed among Fireproof's published modules.

Which one should a department pick?

If voice-driven narrative dictation and an AI-facilitated after-action review process are the priority, and unpublished pricing from a newer vendor is acceptable, Fireproof is worth a look. If the priority is a full RMS platform, water supply, pre-plans, billing, apparatus, personnel, with AI features for onboarding, form building, and narrative drafting bundled into one transparent all-in price, and a vendor with departments already submitting live NERIS data in production, LadderOps is built for that.
