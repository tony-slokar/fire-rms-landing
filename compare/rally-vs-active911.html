Rally vs Active911: Response Coordination App Comparison

Two ways to get a dispatch alert in front of your crew. One prices by member, the other by device. Here's what each includes and what that pricing difference actually means for a department.

Rally and Active911 solve the same core problem: getting a dispatch alert to your members' phones fast, with a way to see who's responding. Both plug into your existing CAD rather than asking you to replace it. Where they differ is pricing structure and what else ships alongside the core alerting app. Active911 has been a fixture in the volunteer fire service for a long time, particularly for departments without a modern CAD/MDT setup. Rally is newer, built as part of a full RMS platform, and priced on a flat per-member basis instead of per device.

Per member vs per device: what it actually means

This is the biggest structural difference between the two products. Rally charges $15 per member per year, flat, no matter how many phones, tablets, or apparatus mounts are running the app. Active911's ActiveAlert is priced per device, on a sliding scale published at active911.com/pricing: $16.65 per device per year for 1 to 50 devices, stepping down to $15.00 for 51 to 100, $14.60 for 101 to 500, and $14.10 per device per year at 500 or more devices.

The practical effect depends on your department's device-to-member ratio. If every apparatus carries a mounted alerting device in addition to personal phones for each member, your device count runs higher than your member count, and a per-device bill grows accordingly. If you're conservative about device count, active on fewer devices than you have members, a per-device model can come out cheaper than a flat per-member rate. Neither structure is wrong. It's worth actually counting your devices against your roster before comparing the two numbers directly.

Standalone alerting vs a path to a full platform

Active911 has built out its own suite around ActiveAlert, ActiveTeam for scheduling and ActiveComms for turning phones into radio communication tools through their ActiveBridge hardware. Those are useful additions if alerting is the only piece you need from a vendor and you're comfortable adding separate line items as you grow into more of their suite.

Rally takes a narrower core (alerts, status, messaging) and leaves scheduling, certifications, NERIS reporting, billing, and the rest to the full LadderOps Platform, starting at $50 per member per year, all-in, no separate line items for each module. If a department wants to stay lean today and knows it may want everything under one vendor later, that's the more direct path. If a department only ever wants the alerting piece and is fine layering on Active911's other products individually as needed, that's a reasonable route too.

Choose Rally if…

Choose Rally if…

  • You want a flat, predictable per-member price with no device tiers to track
  • You want custom status vocabulary your department defines, not a fixed set of labels
  • You want messaging and broadcasts included, not a separate add-on with hardware
  • You'd rather have a clear path to a full RMS under one vendor if you decide to add it later

Choose Active911 if…

  • You already run Active911 and it's working well for your department
  • You want a hydrant location log built into the core alerting app
  • Your device count is lower than your member count, where per-device pricing works in your favor
  • You need Windows desktop client support today, which Rally doesn't offer
  • You want radio-to-phone bridging (ActiveComms/ActiveBridge) as a discrete add-on from the same vendor

Frequently asked questions

Is Rally a replacement for Active911?

It can be, for departments that want a modern, flat-priced coordination app. Rally handles CAD dispatch alerts, one-tap response status, a live status board, and department messaging. It works with your existing CAD rather than replacing it, the same integration model Active911 uses.

How does Rally's pricing compare to Active911's?

Rally is a flat $15 per member per year, published on our site with no tiers. Active911's ActiveAlert is priced per device on a sliding scale, from $16.65 per device per year for 1 to 50 devices down to $14.10 per device per year at 500 or more devices, per active911.com/pricing.

What does per-device pricing mean for a department with more devices than members?

If a department runs one alerting device per apparatus plus personal phones for every member, device count can exceed member count, and the per-device bill grows with it. A department with fewer devices than members can come out ahead on a device-based model. It depends entirely on your device-to-member ratio, which is why we lay out both pricing structures rather than a single number.

Does Rally include scheduling or radio-to-phone communication like ActiveTeam and ActiveComms?

Not today. Rally covers dispatch alerts, status tracking, and messaging. Scheduling and certifications live in the full LadderOps Platform at $50 per member per year. Active911 sells scheduling (ActiveTeam) and radio-bridge hardware (ActiveComms) as separate add-on products.

Does Rally work without a modern CAD system?

Rally connects to your existing CAD to pull dispatch alerts, it does not require you to replace your CAD or MDT setup. Departments running older or manual dispatch workflows should confirm their CAD's alert integration path before switching.

Is Rally available on Android?

iOS is live on the App Store today. Android is coming, and a web app is included in the meantime. Active911 currently covers iOS, Android, a Windows desktop client, and a web admin interface.
