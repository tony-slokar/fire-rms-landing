LadderOps vs ESO Fire RMS

Both are records management platforms built to run fire department operations end to end. Here's how founding pricing and an all-in platform compare to ESO's fire RMS, on NERIS, EMS charting integration, and what's actually included.

LadderOps and ESO Fire RMS are both records management platforms built to run fire department operations end to end: incidents, personnel, inspections, assets, and hydrants in one system. ESO is also a well-known name in EMS software through its separate EHR/ePCR product, which matters if your department already runs EMS charting through ESO. The differences worth understanding before you evaluate either one are how NERIS support is delivered, what integration with EMS charting actually looks like, and how each vendor prices the platform.

How NERIS reporting compares

ESO states on its site that it holds the NERIS V1 Compatible Badge and that there's no additional cost for its NERIS-compliant Fire Incidents application. ESO also states that entering data once auto-generates both a NERIS report and an EHR report. Those are ESO's own published claims. LadderOps' NERIS submission is similarly included at no extra cost, and it's live in production today, not a pilot, with two Wisconsin fire departments submitting real incident data through the platform right now, inside the same record used for personnel, apparatus, and billing.

EMS charting: a real ESO advantage if you're already on ESO EHR

ESO is also a well-known name in EMS software through its separate EHR/ePCR product, widely used by EMS agencies for charting. ESO Fire RMS integrates with ESO EHR, and per ESO, entering data once drives both a NERIS report and an EHR report from the same record. If your department already runs ESO EHR for EMS charting, that's a genuine, practical reason to look at their Fire RMS as a matching product from the same vendor instead of running fire records and EMS charting through two different companies. LadderOps doesn't include EMS charting or ePCR as part of the platform today, so if that integration is what you need, ESO's existing footprint is a real consideration.

Pricing: one published number vs a quote

LadderOps publishes one number: $50 per member per year, all-in, covering NERIS reporting, billing, water supply, pre-incident plans, inspections, personnel, apparatus, AI onboarding, AI form builder, AI narrative assist, reporting, and Rally response coordination. Founding departments lock in $25 per member per year for the life of their subscription. Optional add-ons, Quartermaster asset management and Controlled Substances tracking, are each $500 per department per year, kept separate from the base price. ESO Fire RMS pricing is available on request only; there's no published number on their site to compare against, so a direct dollar comparison isn't possible without going through their sales process and getting a quote scoped to your department.

Which one fits your department?

Choose LadderOps if...

  • You want one published, all-in price ($50/member/year, or $25 founding), no quote process required
  • You're a volunteer or combination department that wants NERIS reporting live now, with named AI features
  • Rally response coordination bundled with your RMS under one price matters to you
  • Data export and ownership as an explicit policy, not a support ticket away, matters to you
  • You want cost recovery billing and controlled substance tracking available from the same vendor relationship

Choose ESO if...

  • You already run ESO EHR for EMS charting and want a Fire RMS from the same vendor, with one data entry driving both NERIS and EHR reports per ESO
  • You're a larger or career department comfortable with a quote-based enterprise procurement process
  • You want a long-established, broad-footprint vendor across both fire and EMS software
  • Digital inspection checklists across vehicles, personnel, and equipment condition matter to your workflow

Frequently asked questions

Does LadderOps hold the same NERIS certification as ESO?

ESO states it holds the NERIS V1 Compatible Badge and that there's no additional cost for its NERIS-compliant Fire Incidents application. LadderOps doesn't frame its NERIS support around a certification badge; it's live in production today, with two Wisconsin fire departments submitting real incident data through the platform.

How does ESO's pricing compare to LadderOps?

ESO Fire RMS pricing is available on request only, with no published number. LadderOps publishes one number: $50 per member per year, all-in, or $25 per member per year for early departments on founding pricing, locked for the life of the subscription.

Does LadderOps integrate with an EMS charting product the way ESO does?

Not today. ESO's advantage here is real if your department already runs ESO EHR for EMS charting: per ESO, entering data once generates both a NERIS report and an EHR report from the same record. LadderOps doesn't include EMS charting as part of the platform.

What's included in LadderOps' base price compared to ESO?

LadderOps' $50 per member per year includes NERIS reporting, billing, water supply, pre-incident plans, inspections, personnel, apparatus, AI onboarding, AI form builder, AI narrative assist, reporting, and Rally response coordination, all under one price. ESO's Fire RMS feature list covers incidents, personnel, inspections, checklists, permits, assets, hydrant status, training, and scheduling, but since ESO's pricing isn't published, a direct dollar comparison isn't possible without requesting a quote.

Is ESO a good fit for a small volunteer department?

ESO is a large, established vendor serving departments of many sizes, but its quote-based enterprise procurement process tends to fit more naturally with larger or career departments that are already used to that process. LadderOps was built around the response conditions of volunteer and combination departments running the platform today.

Do both platforms track hydrants?

Yes. LadderOps includes water supply and hydrant management as part of the core platform. ESO Fire RMS includes hydrant status tracking among its listed features.
