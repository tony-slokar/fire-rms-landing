Rally vs IamResponding

Both apps alert your members to a call and track who's responding. Here's how Rally's pricing, features, and upgrade path compare to IamResponding, published feature by feature.

Rally and IamResponding are both response coordination apps built for fire and EMS. Neither one is a replacement for your CAD system or a full records management platform on its own, they sit alongside your existing dispatch setup and give members a fast way to see a call and mark their status. Where they diverge is in pricing transparency, feature set, and what's available if your department eventually wants more than response coordination.

Who's behind each product

RapidSOS acquired IamResponding in 2022. It's a well-documented deal, and part of a broader field-responder-data strategy at RapidSOS that also includes its 2024 acquisition of eDispatches. If you're already inside the RapidSOS ecosystem for 911 data, that ownership history might matter to you.

Rally is built by LadderOps and designed as the entry point into the LadderOps platform. You can run Rally on its own for response coordination, or add the full records management system later without switching vendors or re-entering your roster.

Pricing you can see vs pricing you have to ask for

Rally's pricing is public: $15 per member per year, no sales call required to find out what it costs. IamResponding does not publish per-member pricing for fire departments on its site. That's not unusual for enterprise software, but it does mean you can't budget against it without reaching out first.

Where NERIS reporting stands

NFIRS retired in early 2026, and NERIS is now the only national incident reporting system for fire and EMS. NERIS incident reporting is live in production through the LadderOps platform today, real submissions, not a pilot. IamResponding's public materials reference free NFIRS 5.0 access, but we didn't find NERIS-specific reporting support documented on their site as of this writing. That's a factual gap in what's published, not an accusation, so if NERIS support matters to your department, it's worth a direct question to their sales team.

Choose Rally if…

Choose Rally if…

  • You want published, per-member pricing you can budget against without a sales call.
  • A live status board for chiefs and custom status vocabularies matter to how your department actually runs calls.
  • You want the option to add NERIS reporting, billing, water supply, and the rest of a full RMS later, on the same platform, without switching vendors.
  • You want NERIS reporting that's already live in production today, not something still being built toward.

Choose IamResponding if…

  • You're not ready to move off a workflow built around NFIRS and want a vendor with a longer operating history in that space.
  • Mutual aid interaction across linked agencies, or toll-free call-in for members without smartphones, are must-have features today.
  • You want to stay inside the RapidSOS ecosystem, since RapidSOS also owns eDispatches and other 911-adjacent data tools, and a single vendor relationship across those matters to you.
  • You need Android support today rather than "coming soon."

Frequently asked questions

What's the core difference between Rally and IamResponding?

Both are response coordination apps: they alert members to a call and let them mark whether they're responding. Rally is built as the front door to a full records management platform, with published per-member pricing and the option to add NERIS reporting, billing, and more later. IamResponding is a standalone response app with a longer track record and features like mutual aid interaction and toll-free call-in that Rally doesn't currently offer.

How does pricing compare?

Rally is published at $15 per member per year. IamResponding does not publish per-member pricing for fire departments; you'll need to contact them directly for a quote.

Does either app replace our CAD system?

No, and Rally isn't trying to. Rally works alongside your existing CAD to deliver dispatch alerts, it doesn't replace it. IamResponding's public materials don't detail their CAD integration approach, so ask them directly if that's a deciding factor for your department.

Which one supports NERIS reporting?

NERIS incident reporting is live in production today through the LadderOps platform, which Rally is part of. IamResponding's public materials describe free NFIRS 5.0 access; NFIRS retired in early 2026 in favor of NERIS, and we didn't find NERIS-specific reporting support documented on their site as of July 2026. Worth confirming directly with them if that matters to your department.

Can we upgrade Rally into a full records management system later?

Yes. The LadderOps Platform starts at $50 per member per year and includes Rally plus NERIS reporting, billing, water supply management, pre-incident plans, inspections, personnel, and more. Founding departments lock in $25 per member per year for the life of their subscription, covering the entire platform and every future module.

Who owns IamResponding?

RapidSOS acquired IamResponding in 2022. It's part of RapidSOS's broader field-responder-data strategy, which also includes its 2024 acquisition of eDispatches.
